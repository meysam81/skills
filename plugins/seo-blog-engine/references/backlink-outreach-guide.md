# Backlink Outreach Guide

## Outreach Philosophy

We are asking strangers for a favor. The email must demonstrate value to THEM, not just to us. Every outreach should answer: "Why should they care about including our content?"

## Prospect Qualification

### High-Priority Targets

| Type                | Description                                   | Outreach Angle                                      |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Listicles           | "Top 10 tools for X", "Best Y in 2026"        | Request inclusion with brief value proposition      |
| Resource pages      | "Useful links for Z", curated directories     | Suggest our page as a relevant addition             |
| Roundup posts       | Weekly/monthly industry roundups              | Pitch a specific insight or data point              |
| How-to guides       | Tutorials mentioning our topic without depth  | Offer our deep-dive as a "for more detail" link     |
| Broken links        | Pages linking to 404'd resources on our topic | Suggest our content as a replacement                |
| Competitor mentions | Pages linking to competitors but not us       | Highlight what our content adds that theirs doesn't |

### Disqualify If

- Domain is a link farm, PBN, or scraper site
- Page has no organic traffic (check via DataForSEO)
- Content is irrelevant to our topic (same industry ≠ same topic)
- Site hasn't been updated in 12+ months
- Contact information is unreachable

## Email Template Structure

### Constraints

- **Format**: Plaintext only — no HTML, no images, no fancy formatting
- **Length**: 3-5 sentences max (excluding subject line)
- **Tone**: Professional, genuine, non-pushy
- **No spam words**: Avoid "free", "exclusive offer", "limited time", "guaranteed", "act now"
- **Spintax**: Use `{variant1|variant2|variant3}` for A/B rotation

### Template Anatomy

```
Subject: {Short, specific, curiosity-invoking}

{Personalized intro — 1 sentence referencing their specific work}

{Value statement — why our content is relevant to their page}

{Soft ask — specific, actionable, low-friction request}

{Sign-off — name, title, no lengthy signature block}
```

### A/B Template Strategy

**Template A — Casual/Peer:**
Conversational tone, shorter, peer-to-peer feel. Best for: bloggers, independent creators, small sites.

**Template B — Professional/Formal:**
Slightly more structured, leads with credentials/data. Best for: enterprise sites, publications, established brands.

Both templates should:

- Reference the specific page/article you want a link from
- Explain what your content adds (not just "we wrote something similar")
- Make the ask specific: "Would you consider adding a link in your [section name]?"
- Keep the CTA soft: "If you think it'd be useful for your readers" not "Please link to us"

## Spintax Patterns

| Element    | Example              |
| ---------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Greeting   | `{Hi                 | Hey                               | Hello}`                             |
| Opener     | `{I came across      | I was reading                     | I found}` your article on {{topic}} |
| Compliment | `{really thorough    | well-researched                   | incredibly helpful}`                |
| Transition | `{I noticed          | I thought you might be interested | It occurred to me}`                 |
| CTA        | `{Would you consider | Do you think it'd make sense to   | I'd love if}`                       |
| Sign-off   | `{Cheers             | Best                              | Thanks}`                            |

## Follow-Up Cadence

| Touchpoint  | Timing         | Approach                                                   |
| ----------- | -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Initial     | Day 0          | Full pitch (Template A or B)                               |
| Follow-up 1 | Day 3-4        | Brief bump — "just floating this back up" + one new angle  |
| Follow-up 2 | Day 7-8        | Value-add — share a related resource, stat, or insight     |
| Follow-up 3 | Day 14 (final) | Graceful close — "no worries if not, thought I'd check in" |

**Rules:**

- Never follow up more than 3 times
- Each follow-up should add something new (not just "bumping this")
- If they respond with "no" — thank them, stop immediately
- If they respond with interest — switch to conversational, drop the template

## Personalization

### Must-Have (every email)

- Recipient's first name
- Specific article/page title you're referencing
- One specific detail about their content that shows you actually read it

### Nice-to-Have (if websearch yields it)

- Their recent work, social posts, or achievements
- A genuine compliment about something specific they wrote
- Shared interest, connection, or mutual reference

### Never Fake

- Don't fabricate compliments about content you haven't read
- Don't claim a connection that doesn't exist
- Don't pretend to be a long-time reader if you're not

## Content Assessment for Backlink Worthiness

Not every page deserves outreach. Prioritize pages that:

| Criterion              | Why                                                     |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Unique data/research   | Original data is the #1 linkable asset                  |
| Comprehensive depth    | 2000+ word guides that genuinely cover a topic          |
| Visual assets          | Diagrams, infographics, charts that others want to cite |
| Practical tools        | Calculators, templates, checklists with real utility    |
| Contrarian/fresh angle | Takes a position others haven't — sparks citation       |
| High search intent     | Pages targeting keywords with business value            |

Skip outreach for:

- Thin content (< 800 words with no unique angle)
- Pure product/sales pages (nobody links to ads)
- Outdated content (unless you plan to refresh it first)
- Duplicate/similar content to what already exists widely

## Metrics to Track

| Metric            | Target        | Description                       |
| ----------------- | ------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Response rate     | 10-15%        | Replies ÷ emails sent             |
| Conversion rate   | 3-5%          | Links earned ÷ emails sent        |
| Domain Rating     | Track monthly | Ahrefs/Moz domain authority trend |
| Referring domains | Track monthly | Unique domains linking to you     |
| Referral traffic  | Track monthly | Visitors arriving via backlinks   |
